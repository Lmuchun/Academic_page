Postgraduate Research program        

Land use change simulation based on CNN-MOGA and Self-correcting CA model

Motivation:

The cellular automata (CA) model can effectively describe, evaluate, and predict land use changes; however, most models use the same conversion rules in the entire study area, ignoring the influence of the spatial and temporal heterogeneity of land use change on the simulation results. In addition, the neglect of model calibration leads to unstable simulation accuracy and insufficient model reliability, which greatly limit the application of the model in actual production. 

Keywords:

Land use change; Cellular Automata; SHAP;
Convolutional Neural Network; Multi-objective Genetics; Automatic Calibration

Methods:

This paper proposes a self-correcting cellular automata model (CNN-MOGA). First, given the insufficient description of spatiotemporal heterogeneity, we divided the space based on the dynamic degree analysis of historical land use; then, the main driving force and land use change of each sub-region (main urban area and remote urban area) were analyzed by the convolutional network; Considering landscape-level and grid-level multi-dimensional evaluation indicators, the multi-objective genetic algorithm NSGA II was then utilized to gain possible CA parameter sets. In terms of degree, compared with the reference model (Future Land Use Simulation, FLUS), the accuracy assessment of simulation results by Kappa and FoM improved by 12.67% and 53.84%, respectively.

Spatiotemporal coupling of vegetation changes and water resources in the Yangtze River Basin and its spatial optimization

Motivation:

Artificial vegetation restoration pathway and water consumption is extremely important for ecological restoration , vegetation restoration structure, and preventing and mitigating floods and disasters. This study focuses on a new quantitative method to clarify trade-offs and synergies between vegetation and water resources and further propose the optimal combination of vegetation and water resources in the basin based on multiple future scenarios.

Keywords:

Vegetation restoration; Water constraint; Ecological productivity; Yangtze River Basin;
Multi-objective optimization

Methods:

Multi-process coupling of dynamic vegetation model and hydrological model: ecohydrological modelling of dynamic vegetation and hydrological processes considering ecohydrological dynamics of the energy balance, water cycle and carbon and nitrogen cycle.

Optimal spatial allocation of vegetation restoration based on multi-objective optimization to explore the optimal scheme under the multi-objective evaluation system and multi-scenarios analysis.

Laboratory Open Fund of Nanjing Beidou Innovation and Application Research Institute

Remote sensing monitoring and model prediction of agricultural and forestry land changes in a complex giant reservoir

Motivation:

Most previous studies focused on land use in the intermittent time nodes of the reservoir area, and less on long-term land use change which led to insufficient understanding of the impacts of hydropower plant project construction on the local natural and socio-ecological environments at long time scales. Thus，we focus on the spatial-temporal change of land use and landscape pattern in giant reservoir clusters at long time scales, which will offer scientific basis for decision-making on constructing urban landscape ecological security patterns.

Keywords:

Land-use simulation; Ecological risk assessment; Driving forces; ​Multi-scenario; Complex giant reservoir

Methods:

Driving force analysis based on the convolutional network to capture spatial-temporal characteristics of land use change.

Use CNN-MOGA model to simulate the land use data in 2025 under three scenarios that are Business As Usual (BAU), Rapid Economic Development (RED), and  Ecological Land Protection (ELP).  

The changes in various types of land and land transfer under each scenario are analyzed. Moreover, use multiple assessment indexes to analyze the ecological risk of land use under each scenario.
